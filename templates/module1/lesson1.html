Lesson Content

Introduction to Molecular Representation

Molecular representation is the process of encoding the structure and properties of a molecule in a format that can be easily processed by computers. There are several common methods for representing molecules, including:

  • SMILES (Simplified Molecular-Input Line-Entry System): A string notation for representing molecules based on their connectivity.
  • InChI (International Chemical Identifier): A standardized, non-proprietary identifier for chemical substances.
  • Molecular graphs: A graph-based representation where atoms are nodes and bonds are edges.

Molecular Descriptors

Molecular descriptors are numerical values that capture various aspects of a molecule's structure and properties. They are used as features for building machine learning models in drug discovery. Some common types of molecular descriptors include:

  • 2D descriptors: Derived from the molecular graph, such as molecular weight, number of atoms, and topological indices.
  • 3D descriptors: Calculated from the 3D structure of the molecule, such as surface area, volume, and shape descriptors.
  • Physicochemical properties: Descriptors related to the physical and chemical properties of the molecule, such as solubility, lipophilicity, and hydrogen bond donors/acceptors.
  • Fingerprints and structural keys: Binary vectors that encode the presence or absence of specific substructures or functional groups in the molecule.
