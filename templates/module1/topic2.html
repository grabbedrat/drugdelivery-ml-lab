Molecular Representation and Similarity Measures

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the concept of molecular representation and its importance
  • Learn about different molecular representation methods (e.g., SMILES, fingerprints)
  • Discover common similarity measures used in drug discovery (e.g., Tanimoto coefficient)

In this topic, we will explore different ways to represent molecular structures and learn about various similarity measures used in drug discovery. Molecular representation is crucial for encoding the structure and properties of molecules in a format that can be processed by computers, while similarity measures allow us to quantify the resemblance between molecules.

Molecular Representation Methods

  • SMILES (Simplified Molecular-Input Line-Entry System): A string notation for representing molecules based on their connectivity.
  • Molecular fingerprints: Binary vectors encoding the presence or absence of specific substructures or features in a molecule.
  • 3D representations: Coordinate-based representations capturing the spatial arrangement of atoms in a molecule.

Similarity Measures

  • Tanimoto coefficient: A commonly used similarity measure based on the overlap of molecular fingerprints.
  • Dice coefficient: Another fingerprint-based similarity measure similar to the Tanimoto coefficient.
  • Cosine similarity: Measures the cosine of the angle between two molecular fingerprint vectors.
  • Shape similarity: Quantifies the resemblance between the 3D shapes of molecules.
