Exploring Chemical Space and Compound Libraries

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the concept of chemical space and its vastness
  • Learn strategies for exploring and navigating chemical space
  • Recognize the importance of diverse and focused compound libraries

In this topic, we will explore the concept of chemical space and its vastness. Chemical space refers to the theoretical space encompassing all possible small organic molecules. We will discuss strategies for exploring and navigating this vast space and the importance of diverse and focused compound libraries in drug discovery.

Chemical Space Exploration

  • Virtual screening: Computational methods to search for molecules with desired properties within a large chemical space.
  • Diversity-oriented synthesis: Generating structurally diverse compound libraries to cover a wider range of chemical space.
  • Fragment-based drug discovery: Using small molecular fragments to explore chemical space and identify novel lead compounds.

Compound Libraries

  • Diverse libraries: Collections of structurally diverse compounds to maximize the coverage of chemical space.
  • Focused libraries: Targeted libraries designed based on specific criteria, such as a particular target or disease area.
  • Natural product-inspired libraries: Compound collections inspired by the structural diversity and bioactivity of natural products.
